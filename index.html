Understanding the Three Levels of Agreement

Explore the general definitions of the three levels of agreement in contracting.

Level 1: Agreed Documents (The "What Documents?") +

Definition: These are the overarching documents that legally or formally bind the parties and outline the consulting engagement. It's about the physical or digital artifacts that capture the understanding.

Examples: A formal Quote or Proposal, a Terms of Reference (TOR) document, or a full-fledged Contract. For less formal projects, a series of emails or a simple non-written agreement can serve as the "document" if mutually acknowledged.

Level 2: Agreed Topics & Policies (The "What Topics are Covered?") +

Definition: These are the broad categories of information that the agreement (from Level 1) is expected to contain, ensuring a shared understanding of the project's framework.

Examples: The agreement should generally include topics like Context, Services and Deliverables, Approach and Work Plan, Roles and Responsibilities, Acceptance Criteria, Terms and Conditions, and various Policies (e.g., regulatory framework, communication, risk management).

Level 3: Agreed Detailed Content (The "What Specifics within Each Topic?") +

Definition: This is the most granular level, diving into the specific information and details within each of the topics identified in Level 2. It's about filling in the blanks with precise data and clear descriptions.

Examples (for "Context" topic): An accurate description of the client's current organizational situation, specific objectives for the assignment, a clear explanation of *why* the work needs to be done, any assumptions made and their potential impact, and the precise scope and explicit limits of the assignment.
